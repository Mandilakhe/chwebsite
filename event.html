UPCOMING EVENTS

  

  Prof. Andile Ngcaba’s great passion for working with the youth of South Africa, and the Eastern Cape in particular, has   recently translated itself into a venture called The Cortex Hub, a technology incubator and research laboratory based in
  East London’s Industrial Development Zone. The aim of the Hub is to bring together bright minds from all over the Eastern   Cape and assist them in the establishment of their own businesses within the ICT sphere. We aim to establish a robust   intelligentsia specialising in cutting edge technology at a global leadership level. To this he would like to announce a   collaborative project between the Computer Science departments of our four major partner universities in the Eastern
  Cape province and the Cortex Hub. This collaborative project be in the form of a Hackathon where each university will be   represented by 5 teams comprising of 3 to 4 people. To stand a chance to win a R100 000 cash prize. Each team will be   required to create an innovative solution that will address the issues we find with the energy crisis currently faced by South   Africa. This Hackathon will make use of the Raspberry Pi, and consist of two showcases:

  •   An initial showcase that will be done on the 18th of July, where entrants will be required to show an initial prototype for
      their inventions.
  •   The second and final showcase date will be on the 25th and 26th of September where the final versions
      of the inventions will be showcased during the Cortex Hub’s first hackathon and also be when the judging and prize giving
      will occur.

  We believe this will be a great opportunity for all entrants in terms of innovation and having an opportunity to see what the
  Cortex Hub does “up close and personal”. As the Hub’s annual hackathon will also be a meeting ground for executives, it
  will be a good opportunity for the students, and their respective universities to showcase themselves. It is also an honour,
  on our part, to get the chance to work on a collaborative project with the institutions responsible for producing the talent
  we are currently working with. We hope that this mutually beneficial relationship will continue well into the future.
